FAQ

How do it use the simulator?
  • For each of the two armies choose the number and unit type of all units that are fighting in the respective army.
  • Decide whether ranged units will use Hit&Run tactics during the battle - and if so, to which extent.
  • Determine whether gold is of higher worth than food and wood - and if so, to which extent.
  • Choose how often the simulation shall be executed.
  • Click the "Start Simulation" button.

I don't want to watch the entire video. How are the units placed right before the battle starts?

This graphic demonstrates the positioning of two exemplary armies:

  • The program first distinguishes between units that have a range of at most one (so for example champions, halberdiers but also step lancers and kamayuks) - let's call them LowRangeUnits - and units with a higher range (so archers, skirmishers but also throwing axemen and gbetos) - let's call them HighRangeUnits.
  • The units of player 1 are on the left side of the map, so they initially have negative x coordinates and the units of player 2 are on the right side of the map, so they initially have positive x coordinates.
  • To make a compromise between very cramped and very open battles all units in the same group will be placed with a distance of 1 tile to the next unit.
  • Low Range units will be placed in a group two tiles away from the middle of the map, and hence close to the enemy. High Range units will be placed in a group six tiles away from the middle of the map, so that they can make use of their range.
  • Regarding the formation, both, the Low Range and the High Range groups of both players have approximately a height double as high as their width.
I don't want to watch the entire video. How does the Hit&Run mode work?
  • If you take the default setting "No Hit&Run", ranged units won't use Hit&Run at all - however, they might still move to get in range of their targets or, if they have a minimum range like Skirmishers, to get out of it.
  • If you take the "Perfect Hit&Run" setting, ranged units will hit & run their targets with absolute perfect efficiency. What does that mean? Let's take the arbalester as an example: Arbalesters fire their arrows after 0.35s of attack animation time and with Thumb Ring they have an attack rate of 1.7s. So with perfect Hit&Run they will use the remaining 1.7s-0.35s, so 1.35s to run away from their target before starting the next attack. This, of course, is barely realizable for humans, even pro player. Therefore there is a third Hit&Run setting called "Medium Hit&Run".
  • If you take the "Medium Hit&Run" setting, Ranged units will run away from their targets with half of the maximal possible efficiency. So compared with the previous example, the Arbalester needs 0.35s to fire his arrow but now uses half of the time until his next attack to run away; so after his attack, he will stand still for 1.35s/2=0.675s and then run away for 0.675s before firing his next arrow. This is still probably at least as efficient as the microing of the average human player.
I don't want to watch the entire video. How close is the simulation to the real game?

This simulator takes all characteristics, properties and attributes of each unit type into consideration, including the following:

  • Hit points & Hit point regeneration
  • Attack values & Armor classes
  • Attack speed
  • Maximum & minimum attack range
  • Attack (animation) delay
  • Projectile speed
  • Cleave damage (different types and radii)
  • Accuracy
  • Missile flight distance (Scorpions, Ballista elephants)
  • Secondary attack projectiles (Chu ko nu, Kipchak, ...)
  • Movement speed
  • Unit radius (size)
  • Resource costs
  • Name
